## Summary

Asana formally rebrands from project management to agentic work management, launching
Asana Dash, AI Teammates, and integrating StackAI. The product addresses four barriers
to AI adoption: discovery, collaboration, context, and governance. Built on 15 years of
work graph data with an established enterprise customer base. The announcement signals
that the industry has arrived at the same conclusion eco has been architecting toward:
a unified operating layer for human-agent teams is the next platform layer.

## Key Points

- **Asana's four barriers to AI adoption**: discovery, collaboration, context, governance —
  eco's primitives already address all four through context inheritance, decision lineage,
  agent onboarding, cross-agent handoff protocols, and multi-agent constitutional alignment
- **Product vs. framework distinction**: Asana Dash and AI Teammates are implementations
  inside one ecosystem; eco is cross-platform, cross-agent, cross-domain, governance-first,
  and designed to be a standard not a product
- **Scope difference**: Asana solves the "AI productivity gap" within enterprise workflows;
  eco addresses the "agentic coherence gap" across biological, cybernetic, AI, and
  organizational systems
- **StackAI acquisition**: signals Asana is buying capability it cannot build fast enough
  internally — the market is moving faster than incumbents can organically develop
- **Market timing**: the industry is now primed; the argument that an OS layer is needed
  no longer has to be made — Asana is making it for everyone
- **Eco as meta-layer**: eco can plug into Asana rather than compete; eco is the thing
  Asana would eventually need to adopt as the underlying standard

## Relevance to Eco

- **Positioning**: eco is at the "Terraform-before-Terraform" stage — the standard before
  the standard exists; Kubernetes, React, and Terraform all felt like "behind" until the
  world caught up; eco's governance-first approach is now a demonstrated market differentiator
- **governance-commons**: Asana is only now talking about governance; eco has been
  architecting it as a first-class primitive — this is a concrete moat
- **agent-dossier / agent-matrix**: eco's agent onboarding, handoff protocols, and
  multi-agent constitutional alignment are ahead of what Asana is shipping
- **cockpit**: eco's cross-platform, cross-domain UI layer can surface into Asana-style
  workflows as a drop-in governance and coordination substrate
- **Standard vs. product decision**: eco's multi-domain scope and modularity position it
  as a protocol/standard (like HTTP or OAuth) rather than a product; this is the key
  strategic fork to resolve

**Big takeaway**: this article is industry validation. The market is now naming the exact
problem eco was built to solve. Being early without resources is not the same as being
wrong — the timing is now correct.
