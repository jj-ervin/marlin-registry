## Overall Summary
The conversation centers on the International AI Safety Report 2026 and its implications for eco’s architecture. The report identifies a global imbalance: heavy investment in preventive safeguards (testing, alignment, regulation) but insufficient crisis‑response infrastructure. It documents real‑world AI failures, reliability gaps, and fragmented crisis handling across regions. This aligns directly with eco’s governance‑first philosophy and validates eco’s need for crisis‑mode architecture, fallback modes, traceability, multi‑agent coordination, and explicit failure‑handling semantics. The conversation integrates eco’s existing primitives—trust semantics, mode systems, reproducible workflows, multi‑engine reasoning—and expands them with crisis governance, de‑escalation, recovery semantics, and structured operational envelopes.
