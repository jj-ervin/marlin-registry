### 1. Measuring Progress Toward AGI: A Cognitive Framework (DeepMind PDF)
**Source:** Google DeepMind  
**Summary:**  
Introduces a 10‑faculty Cognitive Taxonomy for evaluating AGI progress (Perception, Generation, Attention, Learning, Memory, Reasoning, Metacognition, Executive Functions, Problem Solving, Social Cognition). Proposes a 3‑stage evaluation protocol: cognitive assessment, human baselines, and cognitive profiles. Emphasizes held‑out tasks, independent verification, uncertainty quantification, and system‑level evaluation. Discusses limitations of existing benchmarks, importance of processing speed, system propensities, creativity, and end‑to‑end deployment evaluations.  

The 10‑Faculty Cognitive Taxonomy
1. Perception
The ability to extract and process sensory information from the environment.
2. Generation
The ability to produce outputs such as speech, text, motor actions, or computer‑control actions.
3. Attention
The ability to focus cognitive resources on specific aspects of stimuli, thoughts, or task demands.
4. Learning
The ability to acquire new knowledge, skills, or understanding through experience, study, or instruction.
5. Memory
The ability to store and retrieve information over time.
6. Reasoning
The ability to draw valid conclusions and make inferences by applying logical principles.
7. Metacognition
Knowledge about one’s own cognitive processes and the ability to monitor and control them.
8. Executive Functions
Abilities that support goal‑directed behavior, including planning, inhibition, and cognitive flexibility.
9. Problem Solving (Composite Faculty)
The ability to find effective solutions to domain‑specific problems by combining multiple faculties.
10. Social Cognition (Composite Faculty)
The ability to process and interpret social information and respond appropriately in social contexts.
