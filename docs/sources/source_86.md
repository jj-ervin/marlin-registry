### eco-goalie: Defensive Autonomy Layer, Constitutional Safety, and Governed Physical Interception of Machines

**Source:** Copilot discovery card (self-contained synthesis)

**Summary:**
eco-goalie is a proposed eco subsystem: a governance-first defensive autonomy layer that
coordinates defensive robotics for physical interception of autonomous machines (not people).
Core framing: eco-goalie is a robotic shield, not a weapon; a collision-tolerant blocker,
not a hunter; a safety system, not an offensive system. eco acts as the governance brain —
the referee, not the striker. The physical interception mechanism is outside eco's scope;
eco governs when, why, and whether interception is allowed. Four-layer architecture: (1)
detection and classification, (2) decision and governance via eco's constitutional layer,
(3) multi-agent coordination across sensors and nodes, (4) physical interception (mechanism
outside eco's design scope). Constitutional compliance maintained through: defensive-only
scope, machine-not-human targeting, non-escalation enforcement, proportionality, abort
conditions, and complete audit trails. eco-goalie is to autonomous threats what airbags are
to car crashes, firewalls are to networks, and goalies are to soccer.

**Four-layer architecture:**
- Detection and Classification — multi-sensor fusion, trajectory prediction, object classification, adversarial spoofing resistance; eco determines threat class, approach vector, and interception safety
- Decision and Governance — eco enforces human-in-the-loop, constitutional ceilings, non-escalation, machine/human distinction, proportionality, abort conditions, audit logs; eco cannot attack, escalate, target humans, or act offensively
- Coordination Layer — multiple defensive robots, multiple sensors, multiple eco-spark nodes, shared real-time maps, safe interception corridors; eco-spark on DGX provides unified memory, high-bandwidth NVLink, multi-agent arbitration, real-time inference
- Physical Interception Layer — mechanism outside eco's design scope; conceptual options: collision-tolerant drone, blocking robot, net-deploying interceptor, shield device, ground-based blocker; eco governs the decision, not the kinetic mechanism

**Conceptual Signals for eco:**

1. **eco as governance brain, not kinetic actor** — eco-goalie's defining constraint is that
   eco owns the decision layer (when, why, and whether interception is allowed) but not the
   physical mechanism; this separation is constitutional: eco governs, it does not act
   kinetically; the same principle applies across all eco deployments — eco governs agents,
   it does not become the agent; defensive robotics is the physical-world expression of the
   governance-over-autonomy principle

2. **Constitutional ceilings for physical autonomy** — eco-goalie enforces the same
   constitutional primitives as eco's digital layer: cannot attack, cannot escalate, cannot
   target humans, cannot act offensively, cannot exceed declared defensive scope; abort
   conditions halt interception if parameters are crossed; the constitution is not weakened
   by physical stakes, it is strengthened by them; high-consequence physical environments
   require more governance, not less

3. **Machine/human distinction as a first-class invariant** — eco-goalie targets machines,
   never people; this distinction is a constitutional invariant, not a runtime check; the
   classification layer must achieve confident machine identification before any interception
   decision is valid; if classification is uncertain, the default is no-action; certainty-
   threshold before action is a governance principle that applies equally in digital and
   physical domains

4. **Multi-agent coordination for defensive swarms** — eco-spark on DGX Spark provides the
   physical substrate for eco-goalie's coordination layer: unified memory, high-bandwidth
   NVLink, multi-agent arbitration, real-time inference; multiple defensive units share
   real-time situational maps and safe interception corridors; the swarm topology from
   source_81 (swarms as the default execution topology) applies directly to physical
   defensive coordination

5. **Audit trails as constitutional requirements in physical operations** — every detection,
   classification decision, governance decision, and interception outcome is logged with
   full context; audit trails are not optional in physical-consequence systems; they are the
   mechanism by which human oversight remains meaningful even when machine response speed
   exceeds human reaction time; post-incident review uses the same unified postmortem schema
   as digital incidents (source_80)

6. **eco-goalie as the anti-weapon** — eco-goalie is designed to reduce the danger of
   autonomous machines, not to weaponize eco; a system that prevents harm, enforces human
   authority, and cannot be turned into an offensive tool is constitutionally consistent;
   the defensive-only constraint is not a limitation, it is the design

**Constitutional compliance checklist for eco-goalie:**
- Defensive only: yes
- Targets machines, not people: yes (enforced as classification invariant)
- Prevents harm: yes (that is the mission)
- Enforces human authority: yes (human-in-the-loop for all interception decisions)
- Cannot escalate: yes (constitutional ceiling)
- Cannot be weaponized: yes (scope is physically bounded to interception, not attack)
- Audit trail: yes (full log of every decision and outcome)
